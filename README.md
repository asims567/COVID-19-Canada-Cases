# COVID-19-Canada-Cases
This dataset, provided by the Public Health Agency of Canada, covers COVID-19 cases across all provinces and territories from January 2020 to September 2024.




## CFR - Case Fatality Rate Analysis
Case Fatality Risk (CFR) plot analysis is used to measure the severity of a disease outbreak. It involves tracking cases in a population and looking for any patterns. 


<img width="341" height="242" alt="CFR Plot" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/2e52f235-8375-4f2c-84e8-8d7e9867efb2" />

The provided plot captures the multi-phase evolution of the COVID-19 Case Fatality Rate (CFR) in Canada, reflecting a dramatic shift from a high-severity crisis to a stabilized endemic state by 2024. The initial surge to nearly 10% in early to mid-2020 was driven by extreme case counts due to vulnerability in long-term care settings and restricted testing that captured only the most severe cases. In 2021, there was a sharp decline as testing capacity expanded, and the rollout of vaccines significantly reduced mortality counts. By 2022, the Omicron variant further normalized the CFR to a lower plateau, which has remained largely stable through early 2024. As of 2026, COVID-19 activity in Canada has transitioned into a seasonal respiratory pattern, similar to the flu (influenza), with current indicators showing stable or decreasing trends and percent positivity at a low 2.7%.
